Accupressure Techniques

Acupressure is a traditional Chinese healing method that involves applying pressure to specific points on the body to stimulate the body's natural self-healing abilities. It is based on the concept of Qi (vital energy) flowing through pathways called meridians. By pressing on these acupressure points, practitioners aim to restore balance and improve health.

Thumb Pressing:

Technique: Using the thumb to apply steady pressure on a specific point for a few seconds to minutes. Example: Pressing the LI4 (Hegu) point, located between the thumb and index finger, can help relieve headaches and stress. Importance: This technique is commonly used to relieve headaches, stress, and facial pain. It can also help with toothaches and neck pain.

Finger Tapping:

Technique: Lightly tapping specific points using the fingertips to stimulate energy flow. Example: Tapping the GB20 (Fengchi) point, located at the base of the skull, can alleviate tension and migraines. Importance: Tapping this point at the base of the skull can relieve tension headaches, neck stiffness, and dizziness. It's also useful for managing stress.

Palm Massage:

Technique: Applying circular pressure using the palm over a larger area of the body. Example: Massaging the ST36 (Zusanli) point, found below the knee, can boost immunity and digestion. Importance: Massaging this point is known for boosting immunity, improving digestion, and increasing energy levels. It's often referred to as the "longevity point."

Knuckle Pressing:

Technique: Using the knuckles to apply firm pressure to deeper acupoints. Example: Pressing the BL23 (Shenshu) point, located on the lower back, can help with back pain and kidney health. Importance: This technique is beneficial for alleviating lower back pain and supporting kidney health. It also helps with fatigue and menstrual pain.
